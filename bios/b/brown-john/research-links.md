# John Brown — Research Links

## Primary / Credible Sources

- **PBS American Experience — John Brown**  
  <https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/americanexperience/films/johnbrown/>  
  Documentary film with extensive background, timeline, and educator resources.

- **National Park Service — Harpers Ferry National Historical Park**  
  <https://www.nps.gov/hafe/learn/historyculture/john-brown.htm>  
  Official NPS biography and historical context of the 1859 raid.

- **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — John Brown**  
  <https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/john-brown/>  
  Scholarly treatment of his moral philosophy, violence, and abolitionism.

- **Library of Congress — John Brown Resources**  
  <https://www.loc.gov/collections/john-brown/about-this-collection/>  
  Primary documents, photographs, and manuscripts from the LOC collection.

- **Encyclopedia Britannica — John Brown (American Abolitionist)**  
  <https://www.britannica.com/biography/John-Brown-American-abolitionist>  
  Reliable overview biography with citations.

- **American Battlefield Trust — John Brown's Raid**  
  <https://www.battlefields.org/learn/biographies/john-brown> 
  Military-historical perspective on the Harpers Ferry raid.
