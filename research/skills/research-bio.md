# Research Bio

Use this skill to research a bite-size biography before drafting. Create or update `research/biographies/<initial>/<slug>/research-links.md` with source links, source quality notes, unresolved questions, and the facts a writer will need for `biography.md`.

---

## Research Goals

- Build a credible source base before writing.
- Separate established facts from interpretation, legend, propaganda, and disputed claims.
- Identify the scenes, choices, conflicts, and contradictions that can carry a narrative biography.
- Leave a clear audit trail so another writer can tell where each major claim came from.

---

## Source Priority

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Primary sources: letters, speeches, diaries, court records, official documents, contemporary newspapers, interviews, memoirs.
2. Institutional sources: museums, libraries, archives, universities, national parks, historical societies.
3. Scholarly sources: university presses, peer-reviewed articles, academic reference works, specialist biographies.
4. Reputable journalism and documentary sources: major magazines, public broadcasters, well-edited longform outlets.
5. General references: encyclopedias and Wikipedia, used as orientation and citation trails rather than final authority.

Avoid relying on listicles, unsourced blogs, content farms, AI-generated pages, or sensationalized summaries except as examples of myths or public memory.

---

## Research Workflow

### 1. Frame the Subject

- Confirm full name, common name, birth and death dates, national or cultural context, and why the person matters.
- Note alternate spellings, titles, pseudonyms, married names, and names in original languages.
- Write a one-sentence research question: "What makes this life worth a bite-size biography?"

### 2. Gather Sources

- Search for institutional, archival, scholarly, and biographical sources.
- Follow citation trails from Wikipedia or encyclopedias to stronger sources.
- Look for at least one source focused on chronology, one on historical context, and one on legacy or controversy.
- If browsing the web, record the access date when a source may change or disappear.

### 3. Evaluate Each Source

For each useful source, capture:

- Title and author or institution
- URL or publication details
- Source type: primary, institutional, scholarly, journalistic, general reference, or popular
- What it is good for
- Any bias, limitation, paywall, bot-blocking, language issue, or factual caution

### 4. Extract Narrative Material

Collect facts and details under these headings:

- **Timeline**: birth, education, career, major turning points, death, posthumous developments.
- **World Context**: the political, social, religious, scientific, artistic, or economic world around the subject.
- **Key People**: allies, rivals, family, patrons, critics, victims, collaborators.
- **Defining Scenes**: vivid moments that could open or anchor the biography.
- **Conflicts**: external obstacles and internal contradictions.
- **Legacy**: how reputation changed over time and who shaped that memory.

### 5. Handle Disputed Claims

- Flag myths, legends, propaganda, and claims that rest on weak evidence.
- Name the sides of a debate and the evidence each side uses.
- Do not flatten uncertainty into false certainty.
- Prefer language like "historians disagree," "contemporary accounts claimed," or "later legend added" when evidence is mixed.

### 6. Leave Open Questions

End the research pass with specific questions that would improve the draft:

- Missing primary sources
- Contradictory dates or claims
- Context that needs expert confirmation
- Controversies that need a more balanced source
- Details that would make scenes more vivid

## Quality Bar

- Include at least 5 credible sources when available.
- Include at least 2 sources stronger than Wikipedia.
- Include failed or blocked sources only when the failure explains a research gap or suggests a later lead.
- If the source base is thin, say so plainly and identify the next best places to search.
- Preserve uncertainty for the writer instead of hiding it.
